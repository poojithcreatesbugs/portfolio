A book about the beautiful, exhausting, confusing ride of being a young adult today. About how confused people create confused emotions — and how naming what you feel is the first step to making sense of it all.

This isn't a self-help book. It's a mirror. The kind that makes you feel less alone at 2am when everything feels like too much. Written for everyone who's ever thought "why does no one talk about this?" — because they do. They just haven't found the words yet.

This book is for the confused, the overwhelmed, and the ones who feel everything deeply. You are not alone in this.

Philosophy

Why I Write

01
To Create Clarity

Emotions are messy. Language makes them manageable. The moment you can name something — the anxiety, the loneliness, the weird joy mixed with grief — it loses a little of its power over you.

02
To Say You're Not Alone

The feeling that nobody else understands is one of the loneliest feelings there is. Every page says: they do. It happens to everyone. You're not broken — you're just in the middle of it.

03
To Make Life Relatable

We're surrounded by confused people giving confused messages about how we should feel. I write to cut through that — to make the interior life visible, valid, and shared.

04
To Express, Not Explain

I'm not here to diagnose or prescribe. I write to express — to let the feeling breathe on the page so that when you read it, something in you recognises itself and exhales.
